Project goals

On 22.04.2022, the digital conference "Chance for Science" was organised by Prof. Carmen Bachmann. With the resulting network of 176 Ukrainian professors, post-docs and doctoral students from more than 30 higher education institutions in Ukraine, two challenges for teaching were identified, for which the University of Leipzig (Prof. Roland Happ & Prof. Carmen Bachmann) offers support through the DAAD project:

Teaching the course "Qualitative and quantitative research methods"

With the support of lecturers from three Ukrainian universities particularly affected by the war, a teaching offer will be coordinated. The lecturers will help with the translation and adaptation of the materials, refer students to the digital teaching offer and help with the implementation of the teaching, as a supplement to the currently heavily restricted teaching. The digital seminar (90 min/week) with an integrated exercise (90 min/week) is aimed at students in the highly frequented educational and social science degree programmes. Arrangements have been made with three Ukrainian universities to offer the course in English and Ukrainian for students in cooperation with the University of Leipzig from September to early December 2022.

Doctoral programme for 20 young academics on the run

In September 2022, doctoral candidates will have the opportunity to present their doctoral thesis at a digital doctoral workshop. Mentors from the Research Academy Leipzig and the scientific community in Germany will be found to offer support on a professional level. A paper development workshop is also offered to support the doctoral students in the preparation of journal articles. The PhD students will be supported with a stipend (200 EUR per month) from July to December 2022.
